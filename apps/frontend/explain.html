Simple guide

How the dashboard numbers are calculated

Revenue growth

We compare the latest sales number with the previous quarter number. Higher sales usually means demand improved.

Example Previous sales: 100 Latest sales: 125 Growth: (125 - 100) / 100 = 25%

Profit growth

We compare the latest profit with the previous quarter profit. This shows whether the company kept more money after costs.

Example Previous profit: 20 Latest profit: 30 Growth: (30 - 20) / 20 = 50%

EPS growth

EPS means profit for each share. If EPS rises, each share earned more than before.

Example Previous EPS: 4 Latest EPS: 5 Growth: (5 - 4) / 4 = 25%

Margin change

Margin tells how much money is left from every rupee of sales after running the business.

Example Previous margin: 12% Latest margin: 15% Change: 15% - 12% = +3 points

PEAD score

The score starts at 50. Stronger sales, profit, EPS, and margin numbers push it up. Weak numbers push it down.

Example Good sales and profit growth can move the score above 70. A score above 70 is shown as Buy Watch.

Confidence

Confidence is higher when the system finds clear numbers in the filing. If it is low, open the PDF and verify manually.

Example Clear table found: higher confidence. Scanned or messy PDF: lower confidence.
